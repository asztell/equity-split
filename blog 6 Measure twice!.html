Measure twice! Team’s output formula.

Posted: January 24, 2017. | By: Sergei Stadnik



Reading time: 4 minutes


It is easy to measure activity. Did you ever try to measure an individual’s output in startup, workplace, company? Measure of the company's productivity: revenues/sales and income. Measure of the team’s productivity: projects delivered, met deadlines, and perhaps ability to put up fires or lack of such.

Let’s tackle measuring individual productivity as it is harder to define: hours, sales, units of sales, tasks, projects, writing an article and a number of readers and etc.

Solution(s) to issues

Andrew S. Grove, a great CEO of Intel, wrote in his "High Output Management" book:
"The first rule is that a measurement—any measurement—is better than none. But a genuinely effective indicator will cover the output of the work unit and not simply the activity involved. Obviously, you measure a salesman by the orders he gets (output), not by the calls he makes (activity). The second criterion for a good indicator is that what you measure should be a physical, countable thing."

Such indicators have many uses. First, they spell out very clearly what the objectives of an individual or group are. Second, they provide a degree of objectivity when measuring an administrative function. Third, and as important as any, they give us a measure by which various administrative groups performing the same function in different organizations can be compared with each other.

First thing that comes to the mind is work hours. A person spends 40 hours of work which is a perfect definition of activity. How do we know if these hours were spent productively? How do you measure that a person delivers work,or spends his day walking back and forth to Starbucks or checking out Facebook? How do you measure output of the work unit?

That brings us to measurement: various tasks and work completed that add value to the business. It is defined as creating a workflow that allows business to be profitable and measurable behavior that keeps the company afloat. It is computed by the accurate completion of milestones and output delivery.

Lets define output for a typical four member team or startup: leader/manager, analyst, developer and sales person.

1. The Leader / manager

Successful execution requires significant discipline. There is a high chance that the leader’s vision is focused on key issues. If the leader is able to organize people, processes and workflows, then results will be more likely outstanding.

The leader role and output is perfectly defined by Andrew S. Grove:
“We can sum matters up with the proposition that: A manager’s output = The output of his organization (startup or team) + The output of the neighboring organizations under his influence. The definition of “manager” should be broadened: individual contributors who gather and disseminate know-how and information should also be seen as middle managers, because they exert great power within the organization. But the key definition here is that the output of a manager is a result achieved by a group either under her supervision or under her influence.”

The leader's output will be measured by productivity of the analyst, the developer and the sales person. It takes an entire team to win.

2. The Analyst

A person who is analyzing data, specific information, developing solutions to business problems, or to advancing a company's sales efforts. A typical equity analyst would be looking into the company's financial information, industry and economic data in order to price securities. Output of such an analyst will be correct price valuations of various companies and frequency of such updates.

3. The Developer

Developers specialize in creating applications. They have to write readable code as a courtesy to their colleagues, handle debugging and ensure fast execution of such applications by constantly optimizing and refactoring the code.

4. The Salesperson

Such an individual is the easiest to define: a person who sells goods and services to customers. The output is measured by the amount of sales he or she is able to make during a given period. Compensation can be decreased or increased based and directly related on the amount of goods or services sold.

A tracking method ... ✔... ✔... ✔... gives a good understanding of performance and allows the team to build a sustainable company framework. Summarizing what was said above, these factors are standing out:

  • Work hours
  • Task and projects completion
  • Sales unit and dollar volume
  • Peer comparison

The complexity of the measurement involves a human factor as well. It is not just a diligent execution of everyday tasks, but also a proactivity, ability to go beyond the routine, being flexible in decisions and thinking, the ability to focus on the right aspects of the work.
